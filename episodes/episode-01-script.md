# Episode 1 Script — What Is Amendment 5?
*Target: 2 minutes. Voiceover. Screencast with slides.*

---

**[SLIDE: TLR — Episode 1]**

There is a constitutional amendment on Missouri's August 2026 ballot.

It is called Amendment 5.

If Missouri voters approve it, it becomes part of the Missouri Constitution — permanently.

---

**[SLIDE: Amendment 5 — what it does]**

Amendment 5 would set Missouri on a path to eliminate the state individual income tax. Completely.

To make that possible, it authorizes lawmakers to expand sales taxes — on goods and services — instead.

The phaseout happens over time, tied to revenue growth. Not all at once.

Here is the part that matters: the amendment creates the framework. The legislature fills in the details later.

---

**[SLIDE: How Missouri pays for schools]**

Missouri funds public schools through something called the foundation formula.

The formula is designed to make sure every district — rich or poor — has enough money to provide an adequate education.

In Missouri, the state income tax is the primary source of money that funds that formula.

---

**[SLIDE: What the amendment does NOT say]**

The amendment does not include a replacement plan.

It does not say which services will be taxed. Your haircut. Your plumber. Your child care. The amendment does not say.

It does not specify tax rates.

It does not explain how lost revenue gets replaced.

Missouri voters are being asked to eliminate the state's primary school funding mechanism — while it is already underfunded — without a defined plan for what comes next.
