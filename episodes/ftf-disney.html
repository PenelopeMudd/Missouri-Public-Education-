First Things First: The Disney World Trip

The Kurt Warner First Things First Foundation is a Christian non-profit founded in May 2001 by NFL Hall of Fame quarterback Kurt Warner and his wife, Brenda Warner. Its longest-running program is an annual, all-expenses-paid trip to Walt Disney World for children with life-threatening illnesses — a program now in its 24th year and one the Warners make in partnership with Make-A-Wish, the Dream Factory, and Give Kids the World.

The name

The foundation takes its name from a postgame interview. After the St. Louis Rams won Super Bowl XXXIV in January 2000 — with Warner named the game's Most Valuable Player — he opened his remarks by thanking his faith "first." The foundation was established the following year as a 501(c)(3) public charity, directing its work toward families and children facing illness, disability, and hardship in communities connected to the Warners: Arizona, Missouri, Illinois, and Iowa.

The foundation's focus on pediatric disability is rooted in the Warners' own experience. Their eldest son, Zachary, sustained a traumatic brain injury as an infant, resulting in cognitive and visual disabilities — an experience the Warners have cited repeatedly as foundational to the causes they support.

The Disney World program

Beginning in 2002, one year after the foundation launched, First Things First started hosting families of critically ill children on week-long, fully funded vacations to Walt Disney World in Orlando. The trips cover the Magic Kingdom, Epcot, Disney's Animal Kingdom, and Disney's Hollywood Studios. Families stay at Give Kids the World Village, a resort built specifically to host children with critical illnesses and their families.

As of 2026, the program has hosted approximately 193 wish and dream children and their families. Kurt, Brenda, and their now-adult children accompany the families personally during the trips.

The Make-A-Wish partnership

Families are drawn primarily from the rolls of Make-A-Wish and the Dream Factory — organizations that grant experiences to children with critical and life-threatening conditions. First Things First doesn't operate as a wish-granting body itself. It functions as a hosting and funding partner: it identifies and sponsors qualifying families and underwrites the full cost of their travel, lodging, and park experiences.

Make-A-Wish and similar organizations maintain the medical eligibility criteria and family relationships. First Things First provides the sponsored trip as a recurring, branded program. Twenty-plus years of the same collaboration distinguishes this from a one-time charitable gift.
