First Things First: The Disney World Trip

The Kurt Warner First Things First Foundation is a Christian non-profit founded in May 2001 by NFL Hall of Fame quarterback Kurt Warner and his wife, Brenda Warner. Its longest-running program is an annual, all-expenses-paid trip to Walt Disney World for children with life-threatening illnesses. The program is in its 24th year and runs in partnership with Make-A-Wish, the Dream Factory, and Give Kids the World.

The name

After the St. Louis Rams won Super Bowl XXXIV in January 2000 — with Warner named the game's MVP — he opened his postgame remarks by thanking his faith "first." The foundation was established the following year as a 501(c)(3) public charity. It serves families and children facing illness, disability, and hardship in communities connected to the Warners: Arizona, Missouri, Illinois, and Iowa.

The Warners' eldest son, Zachary, sustained a traumatic brain injury as an infant, resulting in cognitive and visual disabilities. The Warners have cited this experience repeatedly as foundational to the causes the foundation supports.

The Disney World program

Beginning in 2002, First Things First began hosting families of critically ill children on week-long, fully funded vacations to Walt Disney World in Orlando, covering Magic Kingdom, Epcot, Disney's Animal Kingdom, and Disney's Hollywood Studios. Families stay at Give Kids the World Village, a resort built specifically for children with critical illnesses and their families.

As of 2026, the program has hosted approximately 193 wish and dream children and their families. Kurt, Brenda, and their now-adult children accompany the families personally during the trips.

Partnership structure

Families are drawn from the rolls of Make-A-Wish and the Dream Factory. First Things First does not operate as a wish-granting organization. It functions as a hosting and funding partner: it identifies and sponsors qualifying families and underwrites the full cost of travel, lodging, and park access.

Make-A-Wish and Dream Factory maintain the medical eligibility criteria and family relationships. First Things First provides the sponsored trip as a recurring, branded program.
