# Too Louse Rock — Design Notes

## Core Principle
Show don't tell. Every claim has an example. Every example has a source.

## Format
Each episode has two layers:
1. **Video** — short (1-3 min), screencast with slides, voiceover. Recorded in CapCut.
2. **Article** — written companion with all sourced claims linked to primary sources.

If it's too long, the concept is too dense. Break it into smaller pieces.

## Publishing Rhythm
Two steps ahead — one episode finished before the previous one publishes.

## Content Types

### Episodes
The main path. Numbered, in order. Each one covers one concept, plain language, no assumed knowledge.

### Side Quests
Optional depth. Standalone pieces that go deeper on a single claim or example from an episode. Labeled clearly. You can follow a side quest or skip it and still understand the main story.
- Same visual styling across all side quests so they're instantly recognizable
- Each side quest links back to the episode that spawned it
- Interior label: **"What this looks like in practice"** — this is the recurring voice for show-don't-tell moments, inside both episodes and side quests
- Style: gray left border, light background, set apart from main text flow

### Compare-O-Meter
Visual tools for making big numbers human. 
- Example: What does $9 billion look like? (Mizzou football players, Missouri teachers, local school districts)
- District formula tool: plug in your district, see what a funding cut means in real dollars

## Navigation / Index Structure
- **Episodes** — main path, in order
- **Side Quests** — browsable on their own, independent of episode order
- **Compare-O-Meter** — tools and visualizations

## Visual Language
- Side quests: gray left border, light background, labeled "Side Quest"
- Main text: clean, Georgia serif, black on white
- Sources section at the bottom of every article
- Video placeholder at top of each episode until video is recorded

## Series Throughline
Missouri is a test case. No state has ever made this switch. That's the honest frame — not partisan, puts responsibility on the voter.

## Kim's Style Markers
- Word repetition within a paragraph is intentional when used — it's emphasis, a wink to readers who are paying attention. Don't auto-correct it.
- No word inflation — if it can be shorter, make it shorter
- Keep the audience anchored in Missouri — name the state redundantly and intentionally. Every time we name a mechanism, we name the state.
- The details become the ways to check the work — specificity is how readers verify, not just how writers prove.
- Never say what we don't do. Say what we do and let people decide.

## What TLR Is
- A few things done well, with receipts
- Facts people can verify themselves
- One concept at a time, plain language, primary sources
